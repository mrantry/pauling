A little something on the life of Dr. Linus Pauling

  • 1901 - Linus Pauling is born to Herman Henry William Pauling and Lucy Isabelle "Belle" Pauling.
  • 1916 - Pauling enters Oregon State University to pursue a degree
  • 1916 - 1917 -Pauling is active in campus life at OSU, and seeks a part-time job to support his mother while finishing school
  • 1917 - Pauling is appointed a teaching position at OSU, teaching quantitative analysis
  • 1920 - Pauling becomes aware of the work of Gilbert N. Lewis and Irving Langmuir, and decides to focus his research on atomic structure
  • 1922 - Pauling graduates from OSU and moves to the California Institute of Technology to pursue research
  • 1923 - Pauling publishes his first scientific paper on crystal structures
  • 1925 - Pauling graduates with a PhD from CalTech with honors, and remains to teach chemstry
  • 1931 - Pauling publishes his paper on "The Nature of Chemical Bonding," a paper which was considered to be the "bible" of chemistry
  • 1933 - Pauling is elected to the National Academy of Sciences
  • 1939 - The Nature of Chemical Bonding is published by Cornell University Press
  • 1942-1945 - Pauling works for several national defense contractors
  • 1946 - Following the aftermath of the events at Hiroshima and Nagasake, Pauling is invited by Albert Einstein to work for the Emergency Committee of Atomic Scientists
  • 1948 - Pauling receives the Presidential Medal of Merit for his work against Nuclear Proliferation
  • 1949 - Pauling is elected President of the American Chemical Society
  • 1954 - Pauling receives the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work in the complex molecular bonding of chemicals
  • 1962 - Pauling is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work against Nuclear Proliferation
  • 1973 - Arthur B. Robinson, Keene Dimick, and Linus Pauling found the Pauling Institute of Science and Medicine
  • 1966-1994 - Pauling does extensive research and publishing on the benefits of Micronutrients, particularly Vitamin C
  • 1994 - Pauling dies of natural causes at the age of 93.
